<h1><small alt="Chapter Zero">Chapter .</small><br>Philosophy</h1>

Given the *is-ought problem*, nihilism is the default state of existence. There is no absolute reason to, but often forgotten, <u>it goes both ways</u>, hence **there is no reason to not**. We have wants, so a beautiful truth is that we simply can declare our wants, and work to fulfill them (aka 'meaning of life' or 'purpose'). These wants we follow are called *terminal goals* (TG). Why? Literally, *"because I said so"*.

The (epistemic) progress of human morality can be generalized to expose a convergence on *Autonomy*: One may do whatever they please, *except* directly affect another agent unconsensually. While we don't yet know the rule behind what we consent to, we can usually discern whether we consent to something, as well as predict the consent of others and ask if we are uncertain.

When one can't consent (eg: children; dogs) we should make choices in service of their *probable* wants (eg: positive wellbeing). This leads to my preferred flavor of this moral theory, which has an imperative for one to actively make positive another agent's wellbeing, assuming their own positive wellbeing can be maintained. Mmm, strawberry! This morality may be enforced by setting boundaries. Literally, *"you're not invited to my birthday party"*, or *"what the heck bro"*.
<small>You may see that this approximates our morals well. Footnote *.A* links to my reasoning, discovered issues, and a more thorough.</small>

<h1><small alt="Chapter One">Chapter I</small><br>Hope Risks</h1>

*Hope Risks* (HR) are the risks of no longer being able to progress our terminal goals. In other words, <u>there is no hope left</u>, also known as doom. Within the context of our TGs, HRs are *very bad*, so we should avoid them. The probability of HRs in comparison to how equipped we are to handle them is **unacceptably** high. Project HRPA aims to make humanity competent enough to manage HRs.

Examples include
- *Existential Risks* (x risks)<br><small>We'd go extinct.</small>
  - Nuclear war
  - Engineered pandemics
- *Hyperexistential / Suffering Risks* (s risks)<br><small>We'd rather be extinct.</small>
  - Sadists torturing us indefinetly
  - Factory farming<br><small>This one is happening right now at <u>our hands</u>, to our nonhuman animal friends.</small>
- *Nonbinary*
	- Other intelligent agents with different TGs<br><small>These could be very bad even if slightly different, *especially* in regards to morals.<br>Note these *don't* have to be x / s risks: Imagine us being kept as pets. While we may have positive wellbeing, our other TGs are nulled, which we don't want.</small>
	  - Aliens
	  - AGI

Together, these are all hope risks. It's in everyone's interest to avoid them.

<h1><small alt="Chapter Two">Chapter I.</small><br>Potential Actualization</h1>

*Potential Actualization* (PA) is to positively progress our terminal goals to their *full* potential. 

Imagine your perfect life. Positively progressing our TGs includes that, within the scope of what's moral and physically possible. A reason I work on Project HRPA is because things I want, like a normal childhood, freedom from mental / physical disability, as well as to live life as my true self, weren't afforded to me, and I don't foresee it in the near future (I also don't have access to therapy). 

This is Potential Actualization, and it's in everyone's interest.

<h1><small alt="Chapter Three">Chapter II</small><br>The Plan</h1>

I, and most if not all other humans, are not smart enough to figure out how to mitigate Hope Risks and achieve Potential Actualization on their own. I've tried for *four* years, before realizing that the path forward is <u>plural</u>. People together, specializing, unifying, and forming a greater whole, are enough. 

The first *instrumental* goal that I set in service of our *terminal* goals is to assemble some passionate nerds, so we may set aside our differences and put our heads together to figure out how to work on these, forming **The Project HRPA Council**.<br>So, I call upon you to join me, in making a more hopeful future ;)
